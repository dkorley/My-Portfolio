About Me

My name is Daniel Korley, and I am a dedicated Data Scientist with expertise in Python, R, SQL, and MATLAB. I am currently pursuing a Master of Science in Applied Mathematics at Texas Tech University, where I am also engaged in advanced research exploring the age-dependent polarization of political preferences in Russian society.

My research leverages machine learning to analyze and interpret complex social and political phenomena, particularly how generational experiences shape responses to political shifts. One of my current projects, titled "Ministry of Happiness: Measuring the Age-Dependent Polarization of Political Preferences in a Russian Society Returning to Authoritarianism," employs innovative methodologies to assess public sentiment and state loyalty through indirect measures.

I am deeply passionate about the transformative power of data science and its applications in shaping public policy and advancing societal understanding. My work reflects a commitment to utilizing data-driven approaches to uncover actionable insights and contribute to meaningful change.
